The interactive epic cube.

Discover all the details of the epic cube.

  • 46 t

    The cube contains 44.4 tons of synthetic resin and weighs 46 tons.

  • 8,892 hrs

    The cube was cast in 90 layers continuously over 90 days. 39 workers were employed for a total of 8,892 hours.

  • 43.48 m3

    The cube, including the frame, is 3.10 metres high, 2.55 metres wide and 5.50 meters long.

  • 39 years

    The Cube is one of the first 1979 G-Class models to be cast. The original DNA of the off-road legend is preserved for all time..

Strong genes. Strong stories.

What makes the G-Class a legend? It's the many stories it tells. They're part of its unique DNA. A DNA – stronger than time.

  • The G brothers.

    Alexander and Christoph Siebertz are dedicated to rally sport. They have been taking part in several amateur rallies since the 90s.

  • California
    Dreaming.

    Dream roads and dream beaches along the coast of California and in the middle of it all, a G-Class. Cole Walliser is living the dream.

  • One G, many faces.

    No G-Class is the same as another. Over the years, various G-Class owners have had a wide range of conversions carried out.

  • Following the footsteps of Marco Polo.

    Annette and Stefan Hauser followed the legendary Silk Road right across Asia to Singapore.

  • In the desert with the prototype.

    The porototype of the G-Class underwent its first tests in 1978. See photos of the original G here.

  • Two master artists.

    The artist Ben Daye applide his skill to the 300,000th G-Class, and turned the design icon into a unique work of art.

  • Around the world in 80 tours.

    Martin Breuninger lives the dream of an adventurer. He has toured the globe for many years, and taken the G to the most remote places.

  • Travelling in the name of the lord.

    In 1980 the Pope visited Germany. A converted G-Class served as a reliable companion on his travels.

  • From Hessia to the Cape of Good Hope.

    Danja and Thorsten Kuhn crossed the African continent from north to south in a G-Class converted into a mobile home.

  • Journey to the west.

    The Japanese Taka Koike and Gena, his G-Class, journeyed westward from Vladivostok all the way to Senegal.
